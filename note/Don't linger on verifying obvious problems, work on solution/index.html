Don't linger on verifying obvious problems, work on solution

Marty Cagan mentions that too many product people spend too much on [[Customer Development is a Hypothesis-Driven Approach to Understanding Who Your Customers Are|customer development]] and too little on "[[solution discovery]]".

If the founder knows the problem you're solving. If other people are confident about what the problem is, then don't spend too much time on validating the problem. In the real world, "the clock is ticking" and you need to start designing the solution.
