Asking questions and doubting the beliefs and assumptions of others will help you think more deeply and critically about the world around you. Socrates calls this method of questioning *elenchus* (now known as the the Socratic method).

Some prompts that call help you challenge your own beliefs:

- What evidence do I have to support my belief?
- What if I am wrong about this?
- What are the alternative explanations?

This really ties into building products, where you use [[Problem Hypothesis|hypotheses]] to validate against the [[Customer Development is a Hypothesis-Driven Approach to Understanding Who Your Customers Are |*beliefs* you have of your target customers]].

By actively questioning your assumptions, and by exposing yourself to diverse perspectives, you can find new possibilities and generate [[Becoming an Original Thinker (innovation)|original ideas]].
