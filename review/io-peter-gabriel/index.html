I/O

Peter Gabriel's latest album I/O is a fascinating collection of songs, likely crafted meticulously over an extended period. Intriguingly, after years of anticipation, he has released not one but two versions – or three, if you include the 'in-side mix' variant (in surround sound/Dolby Atmos). With both a dark side and a light side mix, the tracks are undoubtedly superb, though some do evoke memories of his earlier work.

While unmistakably showcasing Gabriel's signature songwriting style, it occasionally veers towards the mundane and predictable. For example, Playing for Time feels a lot like The Flood. Don't misunderstand me; it remains an excellent album with delightful songs. However, one might have anticipated more experimentation in the songwriting rather than merely the production. It seems as though Gabriel played it safe this time around.
